  1. 1. XIX century

    • - Since 1891, the artist of the Imperial Theaters, I. N. Grekov, ran the
      enterprise in the theater.

    • - In the 1899 season, F. Chaliapin and L. Sobinov, the Figne spouses
      and the outstandin baritone L. Yakovlev toured in Odessa.
    • - From 1897 to 1900, under the entrepreneur A.I. Sibiryakov, the
      theater experienced its greatest prosperity.

  2. 2. XX century

    • - After the liberation of Odessa from the Nazi invaders in 1944, the
      opera house continued its work.

    • - From 1958 to 1960, the famous ballerina, People's Artist of the USSR
      Svetlana Adyrkhaeva served in the theater.
    • - In the 1970s - 1980s, the chief conductor of the theater B. I. Afanasyev
      staged “Khovanshchina” by M. Mussorgsky, “La Bohème” by Giacomo
      Puccini, the mono-opera “Lettersof Love” by V. Gubarenko and “Aleko”
      by S. Rachmaninov.

  3. 3. XXI century

    • - In 2007, the theater troupe took part in the Kuressaare Opera Days festival
      (Estonia).

    • - On January 24-25, during the extraordinary 18th session of the UNESCO
      World Heritage Committee, a politically motivated decision was made to
      include the “Historic Center of the Port City of Odessa” nomination on the
      list of World Heritage in Danger. Including the Odessa Opera and Ballet
      Theater.
    • - At the moment, the Odessa Opera and Ballet Theater continues to operate
      despite the war.
